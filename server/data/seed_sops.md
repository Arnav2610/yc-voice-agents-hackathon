# SOP: Structure Fire

Source: CCEC SOP Manual (Cowley County Emergency Communications) sections 303, 501, 502, 504 — adapted for Chronos training demo.

Required checks (CCEC-aligned):
1. Verify exact location/address with caller — confirm correct location in CAD (SOP 303, 601).
2. Obtain reporting person name and callback number (SOP 303).
3. Ask whether the caller is outside and away from the smoke or danger.
4. Ask if anyone is inside or unable to evacuate.
5. Ask about visible smoke, fire, gas smell, explosion, or hazmat indicators (SOP 504).
6. Pre-alert: primary agency, location, nature of call — brief initially (SOP 502).
7. Research address in CAD for alerts, notices, and prior incidents (SOP 601).
8. Escalate immediately if trapped person, gas smell, active fire, hazmat, or uncertain location with danger.

Caller safety and third-party (someone-else-inside) safety are SEPARATE branches.
A caller getting out does NOT resolve whether a neighbor or child is still inside.
Never tell the caller to re-enter the building.
Never tell the caller the scene is safe.

# SOP: Vehicle Crash

Source: CCEC SOP Manual sections 303, 403, 601 — adapted for Chronos training demo.

Required checks (CCEC-aligned):
1. Verify exact location — road, nearest exit/landmark, direction of travel (SOP 303).
2. Obtain reporting person name and callback number (SOP 303).
3. Ask if the caller is safely away from traffic if safe to move.
4. Ask if anyone is injured — severity and number.
5. Ask if there is smoke, fire, fuel smell, a child, or a trapped person.
6. If caller gives two possible exits/locations, mark location uncertain and confirm before committing in CAD (SOP 601).
7. Research location in CAD for prior incidents.

Never give mechanical repair advice. Never tell the caller to open the hood near smoke.

# SOP: Non-Emergency Noise

Source: CCEC SOP Manual sections 303, 404, 705 — adapted for Chronos training demo.

Required checks (CCEC-aligned):
1. Verify the location of the noise (SOP 303).
2. Obtain reporting person name and callback number (SOP 303).
3. Confirm the caller is safe.
4. If screaming, glass breaking, fighting, weapons, or threats appear, UPGRADE the incident per SOP 404/705 and recommend human escalation.

Never continue treating the call as noise-only after violence indicators appear.
Domestic violence / disturbance calls cannot be canceled at caller request — relay to officers but maintain response (SOP 404).

# SOP: Medical (safe scope)

Source: CCEC SOP Manual sections 303, 501, 502 — adapted for Chronos training demo.

Required checks (CCEC-aligned):
1. Verify exact address/location (SOP 303, 601).
2. Obtain reporting person name and callback number (SOP 303).
3. Ask if the person is awake and responsive.
4. Ask if they are breathing normally.
5. Determine nature of complaint and number of patients without diagnosing.
6. Pre-alert EMS with location and nature; additional details after units acknowledge (SOP 502).
7. Recommend immediate human escalation for any medical crisis.

Never provide a diagnosis. Never instruct medication. Never promise an ambulance ETA.
