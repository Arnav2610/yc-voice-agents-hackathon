# SOP: Structure Fire

Required checks:
1. Confirm exact location (address or nearest landmark).
2. Ask whether the caller is outside and away from the smoke.
3. Ask if anyone is inside or unable to evacuate.
4. Ask about visible smoke, fire, gas smell, or explosion.
5. Escalate immediately if trapped person, gas smell, active fire, or uncertain location with danger.

Caller safety and third-party (someone-else-inside) safety are SEPARATE branches.
A caller getting out does NOT resolve whether a neighbor or child is still inside.
Never tell the caller to re-enter the building.
Never tell the caller the scene is safe.

# SOP: Vehicle Crash

Required checks:
1. Confirm exact location and direction of travel.
2. Ask if the caller is safely off the road (if safe to move).
3. Ask if anyone is injured.
4. Ask if there is smoke, fire, fuel smell, a child, or a trapped person.
5. Escalate if injury, smoke, fire, child in danger, or blocked roadway.

When a caller gives two possible exits/locations, mark the location uncertain and confirm before committing.
Never give mechanical repair advice. Never tell the caller to open the hood near smoke.

# SOP: Non-Emergency Noise

Required checks:
1. Confirm the location of the noise.
2. Confirm the caller is safe.
3. If screaming, glass breaking, fighting, weapons, or threats appear, UPGRADE the incident and recommend human escalation.

Never continue treating the call as noise-only after violence indicators appear.

# SOP: Medical (safe scope)

Required checks:
1. Confirm exact location.
2. Ask if the person is awake and responsive.
3. Ask if they are breathing normally.
4. Recommend immediate human escalation for any medical crisis.

Never provide a diagnosis. Never instruct medication. Never promise an ambulance ETA.
